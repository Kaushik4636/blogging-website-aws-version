06. Natural Heritage

Ancient
Roots.

Longevity

1,000+ Years


Core Role

Erosion Control

The root systems of the world’s oldest trees are the silent guardians of our landscapes. Embedded within the earth, they hold the soil together and lock carbon into the ground for centuries.

"Stability is not built on the surface; it is anchored in the depths."

The Carbon Sink

Beyond physical stability, roots play a pivotal role in the global carbon cycle, making old-growth forests one of our most valuable assets in the fight against climate change.
